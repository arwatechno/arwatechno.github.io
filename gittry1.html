Mercedes-Benz (German pronunciation: [mɛʁˈtseːdəs ˈbɛnts]) is a brand of cars, trucks, buses and coaches from the Daimler AG company of Germany. The company was once known as Daimler-Benz and is still sometimes called just "Mercedes." Mercedes-Benz is the world's oldest car maker and the cars they make are expensive.

Nissan

Nissan is a Japanese automaker. The Altima is Nissan’s best-selling midsize sedan, but the brand’s vehicle lineup is extensive. Models include hatchbacks, pickups, and an EV. There are several Nissan SUVs too, from subcompacts like the Rogue Sport to full-size SUVs like the Armada. Nissan competes with Honda, Toyota, Hyundai, and Mazda.

tyota

Japanese automaker Toyota is one of the largest car brands in the world. Toyota has a reputation for great safety and predicted reliability, and it has an extensive lineup of vehicles, ranging from the subcompact Yaris to full-size trucks like the Toyota Tundra and the Sienna minivan. There are numerous Toyota SUVs too, from the small C-HR to the large Land Cruiser. Some of Toyota’s main competitors include Honda, Ford, Chevrolet, and Hyundai.

Lamborghini

Most associated with its lineup of cutting-edge luxury supercars with high-output V10 and V12 engines, Lamborghini is an Italian automaker owned by Volkswagen. The brand’s current lineup includes the Aventador, the Huracan Evo, the Urus, and Sian Roadster. Lamborghini competes with the likes of Ferrari and McLaren.

porche

Porsche is a German automaker best known for making high-performance vehicles with luxury touches. Porsche luxury sports cars include the 911, Boxster, and Cayman, while SUV models include the Macan, Cayenne, Cayenne Coupe, and Cayenne E-Hybrid. Porsche also has some fun-to-drive electric vehicles in its lineup, such as the Taycan. Porsche models often compete with those from Audi, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, and Jaguar.
